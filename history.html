Historic Milestones

Discover the great accomplishments mankind has made regarding robots, and how they will eventually shape the future of robotics, providing a greater quality of life for all.

1961

The first industrial robot, Unimate, was created. It was used to manufacture cars to automate metalworking and welding.

1972

Shakey, the first AI-powered robot, could plan its journey using sensors, a TV camera, a range finder, and touch-sensitive whiskers when given a goal.

This image is from sri.com and was modified

2000

After decades of research, Honda unveiled ASIMO, a 1.3-meter-tall robot that could walk, interact, and recognize objects, body language, faces, and sounds.

2002

The Roomba, the world's most popular cleaning robot, was released in 2002. It detects dirt, navigates around rooms and objects, and can be controlled via a mobile app.

2005

Self-driving cars advanced AI with Stanley, an autonomous Volkswagen created by Stanford. Using 100,000 lines of machine learning code, Stanley won the 2005 DARPA Grand Challenge.

Past To Present

A detailed timeline about robotics from centuries ago to the present, and beyond. Discover how the technology within robotics mutate over time.

1452

Leonardo Da Vinci made one of the first recorded designs of a robot. It looked like a knight with armour. The robot is able to sit, stand, raise its visor, and also control its own jaw.

1928

WWI Veteran, Captain William Richards, built Eric, a robot that could move its hands and head. Eric was able to be activated by voice command. The robot's first appearance was seen at an event, where Eric rose to its feet and gave a four minute speech.

2000

Created by Honda, ASIMO is a robot designed to help people. It is able to walk and climb stairs independently. ASIMO is able to detect surrounding environment with its built-in camera and can also interpret voice commands, building the foundation of AI.

2016

Created by Hanson Robotics, Sophia is a revolutionary artificial intelligent robot. Sophia can engage in conversations and even make expressions on her face. In 2017, Sophia was granted a citizenship by Saudi Arabia.

2024

Although first revealed in 2013, the Atlas robot by Boston Dynamics new improvements make Atlas the most agile and dynamic robot in the world. Atlas can perform complex body movements that allow it to exceed human capabilities. For example, Atlas is able to rotate its limbs 360 degrees.
